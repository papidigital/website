Case Study

Record-Breaking Podcast Marketing - The Donnie Brasco Story

Julian Espinosa / December 2020

Overview

This podcast is centered around Joe Pistone who was the undercover FBI agent that infiltrated the New York Mafia in the late 70s. The goal was to test the market and determine the viability of this podcast. Through this test, we discovered the audience and the type of content that appeals to users.

The results of this test were shocking. Not only did we discover an audience but this campaign broke paid ad records for podcasting. This test sets how podcast marketing will be done in the future. This also happens to a really cool podcast, listen to it if you get a chance!

Market research involved, and how we determined our target audience

We used Audience Insights to find the audience. It was a lot of trial and error. We searched for mafia-related interests to see if Facebook had an audience for it. We searched for law enforcement interests.

Based on several types of approaches we made a “hypotheses” that targeting mob-related movies would be the best performer (it was only an educated guess). We based our selection around 90s mafia movies. We assumed they would have likely seen “Donnie Brasco.”

Audience

Likes: Donnie Brasco (film), Goodfellas, Blow (film) or Casino (film)

And Must Also Match: Interests: Podcast

Detailed Targeting Expansion: Off

Assets we chose and why

Assets were tricky with this campaign. The main reason is that Facebook has been denying a lot of “sensational.” This IP has a lot of drugs, gambling, and murders we needed to be careful with what we selected. The logic was to identify the most provocative clips we could find

At that point, we picked the ones we believed Facebook would approve (again this is a guess). It was a test, but to hedge, we used 5 different clips. We did get lucky and overtime we were able to get them all approved. We did get our fair share of Facebook denials.

Testing and refining of ad sets with examples

We used a 3 stage testing system.

First Stage: We tested out the audience which we called “Mob Movies.” We used only one asset on this which we called “Donnie The Jewel Thief.” It is a 53-second clip where Joe explains how he infiltrated the mafia posing as a jewel thief. Our results netted a $0.02 cost per click in this test. Which was almost not believable. At that point we knew we had the right audience, their was no point in testing any other audiences.

Second Stage: Even though our numbers were really good, it was only on one piece of creative. We knew we need to test out different assets to see what we could similar performance from other assets. To our surprise, all assets performed really well. Our best asset was getting us $0.03 per click and our worst was getting us $0.09 per click.

Third Stage: Now that we found our best-performing assets. The idea was to push those assets over a larger spend to see if it scaled. We used “Donnie The Jewel Theif” and “Mafia’ Guy’s Wife or Daughter” even with scaling the budget these assets held up and didn’t exceed $0.08.

Breathtaking. In 12 years of digital marketing, I haven’t had a perform this well. Let alone right from the start.

  • We have several impactful assets that we know hold up at scale.
